Shoulder Recovery Plan

A printed reference for taking care of him after surgery. Keep it on the fridge.

Surgery: May 11, 2026
Surgeon: Dr. Silver
Hospital: NYU Langone Orthopedic

What happened

Dad fell on March 3 and tore the tendons in his shoulder. These are called the rotator cuff. They are what let you lift your arm.

On May 11, Dr. Silver fixed the tear. Through small cuts in the shoulder, he sewed the torn tendons back to the bone. He used tiny anchors to hold them in place. He also added a patch on top, like a bandage on the inside, to make the repair stronger.

The surgery went well. No problems. Dad came home the same day.

What to expect

  • The shoulder needs 4 to 6 weeks in the sling. No exceptions.
  • Then physical therapy starts and lasts months.
  • Full recovery: 6 to 9 months. Be patient.
  • The biggest risk is moving the arm too soon. If the repair fails, he needs another surgery.

The 4 most important rules

  1. Sling stays on. Day and night. Even sleeping. Only off for showers.
  2. No lifting with that arm. Nothing heavier than a coffee cup.
  3. Pain meds with food. Always. Or he gets sick.
  4. Ice the shoulder. 15-20 min, many times a day for week 1.

A normal day, roughly

Morning
  • Help him out of bed slowly
  • Bathroom, breakfast
  • Morning meds (with food)
  • Pain meds if needed
  • Ice 15-20 min
  • Check fingers look pink
Afternoon
  • Lunch (then pain meds OK)
  • Short walk if up for it
  • Ice 15-20 min
  • Rest in recliner
  • Help with anything two-handed
Evening
  • Dinner with evening meds
  • Ice one more time
  • Pain meds if needed
  • Brush teeth, wash up
Bedtime
  • Pain meds BEFORE sleep
  • Sling stays ON
  • Pillows around the arm
  • Phone within reach

The sling: how to put it on

  1. Sit him down. Arm in his lap or on a pillow.
  2. Slip his elbow into the bend of the sling.
  3. Reach the long strap up and over the OTHER shoulder.
  4. Pull the Velcro through the front rings, stick it down.
  5. Check: elbow at 90°, arm across belly, only wrist and hand showing.

Ice / cooling sleeve

  • 15-20 min on, then off. 3-5 times a day in week 1.
  • If he has the cooling sleeve, follow its directions. Better than ice packs.
  • For ice packs, wrap in a thin towel. Never on bare skin.
  • Best times: morning, before bed, before pain meds.

Showering

  • First 5 days: no showers. Sponge baths only.
  • Day 5 (May 16): Take dressing off, rinse cuts gently, cover with daily Band-Aids.
  • After day 5: Sling off in shower. Arm hangs straight down at his side. You wash him. Sling back on right after.
  • No baths, pools, hot tubs until the doctor says OK.

Things he absolutely cannot do

  • Lift more than a coffee cup with that arm
  • Reach overhead
  • Sleep without the sling
  • Drive while taking oxycodone
  • Drink alcohol while on pain meds
  • Take Advil or aspirin without asking the doctor

All the medications

Two new pain pills to start. Some old ones to stop. Everything else stays the same.

Strong · Use with care
Oxycodone
also called Roxicodone · 5 mg pill
2 pills every 6 hours, only if needed Drop down to 1 pill as pain gets better. Never more than 8 pills (40 mg) in one day. Always with food. Bottle has 30 pills.
Mild · Safer
Tylenol
also called Acetaminophen · 500 mg pill
2 pills every 6 hours, only if needed Use BETWEEN oxycodone doses for steady coverage. Bottle has 60 pills.

The pain plan, simply

Use BOTH together for the first few days. Layer them. Take Tylenol 3 hours after oxycodone. That way he always has something working.

Then taper. Drop oxycodone first, one pill at a time. Keep Tylenol if he still needs it.

Always with food. Even crackers. Empty stomach makes him throw up.

The first night rule

The numbing block from surgery wears off in the middle of the night. Take pain medication BEFORE bed on the first night. With food. Don't skip this. He'll thank you in the morning.

Pills to STOP and KEEP

STOP taking (paused for surgery)

  • Aspirin 81 mg (Bayer)
  • Voltaren 75 mg (diclofenac)
  • Gralise 300 mg (gabapentin)
  • Crestor 10 mg (rosuvastatin)
  • Prilosec 40 mg (omeprazole)
  • Flonase nasal spray
  • Kenalog ointment
  • Alpha Lipoic Acid, Vitamin B

Aspirin and Voltaren make you bleed more. Ask Dr. Silver or Dr. Beker when he can restart these.

KEEP taking as normal

  • Metformin 500 mg — once daily with meals (diabetes)
  • Hyzaar 100/12.5 mg (losartan-HCTZ) — once daily (BP)
  • Toprol XL 25 mg (metoprolol) — once daily (heart/BP)
  • Pletal 100 mg (cilostazol) — twice daily (circulation)
  • Blood sugar checks — same as always

NEVER mix oxycodone with these

  • Alcohol. Even one drink. It can stop his breathing.
  • Sleeping pills (Ambien, etc.)
  • Anti-anxiety pills (Xanax, Ativan, Klonopin)
  • Other pain pills from any source

When to worry

Call 911 / Go to the ER NOW

  • Chest pain or trouble breathing
  • Fingers turn pale, blue, or purple
  • Arm stays totally numb after 48 hours
  • Heavy bleeding from the bandage
  • Confused, very weak, or dizzy
  • Hasn't peed in 8+ hours
  • Can't wake him up or breathing is very slow

Call Dr. Silver: 929-455-2000

  • Fever over 101°F (38.3°C)
  • Chills
  • Cuts get red, hot, swollen, or have pus
  • Strange liquid from the cuts
  • Pain that meds aren't helping at all
  • Throwing up that won't stop
  • Pain or swelling in his calf (could be a blood clot)

Things that look bad but are NORMAL

  • Bruising down the arm. Blood drains with gravity. Can spread to his hand. Goes away in 1-2 weeks.
  • Some swelling. Ice helps.
  • Tingling / pins & needles as block wears off. This is the cue to take pain meds.
  • Constipation. Normal from pain pills. Fluids, fiber, laxative if needed.
  • Trouble sleeping. Recliner helps.
  • Feeling down or grumpy after a few days. Surgery is hard on the spirit.
